1.WHAT ARE GOOGLE MAPS?
  • Google Maps is a web service that provides detailed information about geographical regions and sites worldwide. In addition to conventional road maps, Google Maps offers aerial and satellite views of many locations. In some cities, Google Maps offers street views comprising photographs taken from vehicles.
  • Google Maps offers several services as part of the larger web application. For example, a route planner offers directions for drivers, bikers, walkers and users of public transportation traveling from one specific location to another.
  • Google Maps is a popular web-based mapping service developed by Google. It provides detailed maps and satellite imagery of various locations around the world, along with a range of features and functionalities. Here are some key aspects of Google Maps: Maps and Satellite Imagery: Google Maps offers interactive maps that display roads, landmarks, businesses, and geographical features. Users can switch between different map views, such as standard map, satellite imagery, and street view, to explore and navigate through different locations. Directions and Navigation: One of the primary features of Google Maps is its ability to provide directions and navigation assistance. Users can enter their starting point and destination to get step-by-step directions for driving, walking, biking, or using public transportation. The service also provides estimated travel times, alternative routes, and real-time traffic information. Local Business Information: Google Maps incorporates a comprehensive database of businesses and points of interest. Users can search for specific businesses or browse categories to find nearby restaurants, hotels, gas stations, hospitals, and more. Each listing includes contact information, reviews, ratings, photos, and sometimes even virtual tours. Street View: Google Maps integrates Street View, a feature that provides panoramic views of locations at the street level. Users can explore cities, landmarks, and residential areas by virtually navigating through 360-degree street-level images. This feature offers a more immersive and detailed view of various places.
    • 2. What is the Google Maps API?
  • The Google Maps application program interface (API) allows different software platforms to communicate with each other. For example, website administrators can embed Google Maps into a proprietary site, such as a real estate guide or community service page.
    • 3.How do you download offline maps on Google Maps?
  • Maps can be downloaded for mobile access when a user is offline by tapping on the profile icon and then tapping Offline maps. A user can also tap Select your own map, and Google Maps will suggest maps based on history, upcoming trips and saved addresses. To download a specific map, pinch in and out to set the download area and tap Download.

    • 5.How do you measure distance on Google Maps?
  • To measure the distance between two points -- for example, a user's home and place of work -- open Google Maps and start by right-clicking at the starting point. Next, click Measure distance on the menu, and then click at the destination point to draw a line and measure the distance between both locations.
